The Perfect Homemade Pizza

Crispy crust, melty cheese, and flavour in every bite.

There's nothing quite like a homemade pizza, fresh out of the oven with a golden, crispy crust and bubbling cheese. This classic Margherita-style pizza is simple, flavorful, and made entirely from scratch using fresh ingredients. Whether you're cooking for a family dinner or a cozy night in, this recipe is guaranteed to impress without the need for fancy equipment or hard-to-find ingredients.

Prep & Cook Time:

  • Preparation Time: 20 minutes
  • Rising Time: 30-45 minutes
  • Cook Time: 15 minutes
  • Total Time: Approximately 1 hour 15 minutes

Servings:

Makes 1 large pizza (serves 2-3 people)

  1. Preheat the Oven

    Preheat your oven to 475¬∞F (245¬∞C). If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it heats.

  2. Make the Dough

    In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. In a separate cup, dissolve the yeast in warm water. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour, then mix until a rough dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for about 8-10 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  3. Let the Dough Rise

    Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 30-45 minutes, or until doubled in size.

  4. Prepare the Sauce

    While the dough is rising, mix the tomato sauce, minced garlic, oregano, salt, pepper, and olive oil in a small bowl. Set aside.

  5. Shape the Dough

    Once risen, punch down the dough and roll it out on a floured surface to your desired thickness. Transfer to a baking sheet or pizza peel dusted with flour or cornmeal.

  6. Assemble the Pizza

    Spread the sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Sprinkle generously with shredded mozzarella. Add any toppings of your choice (like pepperoni or mushrooms).

  7. Bake the Pizza

    Place the pizza in the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.

  8. Finish and Serve

    Remove from the oven and let cool for 2-3 minutes. Top with fresh basil leaves and a light drizzle of olive oil. Slice and serve hot!

üí°¬†Tips & Variations

  • Spice It Up: Add a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes to the sauce or sprinkle on top after baking for a spicy kick.
  • Whole Wheat Option: Substitute half or all of the all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour for a healthier, nuttier crust.
  • Vegetarian Friendly: Skip the pepperoni and load up with veggies like bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, olives, or spinach.
  • Cheese Choices: Try mixing mozzarella with other cheeses like provolone, fontina, or parmesan for extra depth of flavor.
  • Make It Personal: Divide the dough into mini pizzas so everyone can choose their own toppings.
  • Crispier Crust: Bake your pizza on a preheated pizza stone or steel for an extra crispy base.
